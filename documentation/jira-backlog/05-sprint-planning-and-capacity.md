# Sprint Planning and Capacity

## Planning Goal

Commit to realistic sprint scope that delivers the highest-priority outcomes with quality.

## Input Checklist

- Prioritized backlog (with scores).
- Team availability for sprint period.
- Carry-over and production support expectations.
- Known dependencies and risks.

## Capacity Model

1. Calculate total team days for sprint.
2. Subtract planned non-delivery time (meetings, PTO, support).
3. Reserve 10-20% for unplanned work.
4. Convert remaining capacity into expected story point range.

## Commitment Rules

- Commit only **Ready** stories.
- Keep at least one small fallback story ready.
- Avoid splitting a story across sprints unless explicitly planned.
- Protect quality work (tests/refactoring) within sprint scope.

## Suggested Sprint Ceremony Flow

1. Review sprint goal.
2. Pull highest-priority ready stories.
3. Validate dependency order.
4. Confirm capacity fit.
5. Record risks and owner for each risk.

## Mid-Sprint Scope Control

- New urgent work requires explicit trade-off.
- Document added/removed scope in sprint notes.
- Preserve sprint goal before preserving initial scope list.

## End-of-Sprint Review Metrics

- Planned vs completed points.
- Scope change count.
- Defect escape count.
- Goal achievement (met/partially met/not met).
